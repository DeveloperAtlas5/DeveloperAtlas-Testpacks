# Frequently asked questions

## Is Atlas an AI coding agent?

No. Atlas defines and records boundaries around AI-assisted work. It does not autonomously own the
goal, run every action, or make the final acceptance decision.

## Does my source code leave my computer?

Current Navigator and Control workflows are local-first and contain no Atlas telemetry. A separate AI
tool may have its own data policy; Atlas cannot change that policy.

## Is the full product open source?

No. This repository is a deliberately limited public preview. Its committed files use the MIT
License; the private monorepo and unreleased product source are separate.

## Can I install Navigator today?

Navigator is working software and a preview candidate, but a generally downloadable, signed public
release is not yet promised here. Follow the tester guide for current preview availability.

## Which stacks are supported?

The strongest current path is Laravel, Blade, PHP, JavaScript, HTML, and CSS. Vue support is partial
and explicitly shown as incomplete in the mission index.

## What is independently reviewed?

Independent human review of the maintained Knowledge Nodes is still incomplete. Public node headers
show primary-source mapping, AI assistance, automated checks, and the pending independent state.

## What does Atlas verify?

Atlas can check declared scope, structured contracts, evidence consistency, and maintained automated
tests. The exact verification depends on the artifact and does not imply universal correctness.

## What does Atlas not guarantee?

Atlas does not guarantee secure, production-ready, legally compliant, or bug-free code. It does not
prove product-market fit, retention, or willingness to pay.

## Why are some parts public and others private?

The public repository is a reviewed demonstration and feedback surface. Private product code,
strategy, raw feedback, and the complete knowledge collection remain behind a one-way export boundary.
